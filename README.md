# Portfolio Projects

## Economic Research & Forecasting

▪︎ **[Master's Thesis — Predicting Firm-Level Performance: Evidence from Italian Private Firms](https://github.com/saidmouhtaram/PortfolioProjects/blob/Python/01_07_2026_Final_Version_Thesis_Said_Mouhtaram.pdf)** *(Python)*  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tested whether productivity growth is predictable from balance-sheet and institutional data for ~14,000 Italian private firms (AIDA). Found that productivity growth is heavy-tailed and largely unpredictable overall, but the tails are asymmetric: top-1% positive shocks are strongly discriminable (AUROC up to 0.93), while bottom-1% collapses are not, with Revenue per Capita, Asset Turnover, and the South Dummy emerging as the most consistent predictive signals.

▪︎ **[Italian Unemployment Prediction Through Google Searches](https://github.com/saidmouhtaram/PortfolioProjects/blob/R/Analysis_SM.pdf)** *(R)*  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nowcasted monthly changes in the Italian unemployment rate using Google Trends data on job- and leisure-related keywords, comparing OLS, Ridge, LASSO, Random Forest, and XGBoost within a rolling-origin cross-validation framework. Random Forest delivered the lowest out-of-sample RMSE (≈0.305), showing that the search–unemployment relationship is meaningfully nonlinear.

## Exploratory Data Analysis

▪︎ **[American Housing Price Analysis](https://github.com/saidmouhtaram/PortfolioProjects/blob/R/housing_analysis_with_output.pdf)** *(R)*  
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Built a multiple linear regression on a Kaggle US housing dataset (545 observations) to identify the drivers of listing prices. Bathrooms, air conditioning, hot water heating, preferred-area location, and main-road access came out as the strongest predictors, with the final model explaining roughly 68% of price variance (R² ≈ 0.68).
